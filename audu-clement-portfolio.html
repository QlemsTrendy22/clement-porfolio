How I work

Design grounded in evidence, built for everyone.

Good instructional design is more than polish. I pair current practice — AI as a design partner, accessibility from the first draft — with the discipline of learning science.

Trend · AI for learning

AI as a design partner

I use AI to move faster — drafting branching scenarios, generating assessment variants, and prototyping interactions — while keeping pedagogy and human judgment firmly in the loop.

Trend · Accessibility

Accessible by default

Every build starts from WCAG and Universal Design for Learning — multiple means of engagement, representation, and expression — so access is designed in, never retrofitted.

Trend · Evidence-based

Research-grounded

Decisions trace back to learning science and the realities of the classroom — informed by research and tested against real contexts, not trends for their own sake.

Design process

The right model for the problem.

I don't force every project through one workflow. I match the methodology to the goal, the timeline, and the complexity of what learners need to be able to do.

Model 01

ADDIE

Analyze · Design · Develop · Implement · Evaluate

A systematic backbone — analysis through evaluation — that keeps complex builds organized and defensible end to end.

I reach for it whenScope is well-defined: full courses, compliance, and structured curriculum work.
Model 02

SAM

Successive Approximation Model

An agile, prototype-first cycle. Short design–build–review loops surface problems early and keep stakeholders close to the work.

I reach for it whenSpeed and collaboration matter, or requirements will evolve as we learn.
Model 03

4C/ID

Four-Component Instructional Design

Built around authentic whole tasks, scaffolded with supportive information, just-in-time guidance, and targeted practice.

I reach for it whenLearners must integrate complex skills — professional and higher-ed training.
